What is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying refers to the use of technology—such as social media, messaging apps, or gaming platforms—to intimidate, harass, or bully someone. It can involve sending threatening messages, spreading rumors, or sharing harmful images or videos. It is a growing problem as more people spend time online.

Effects of Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying can have devastating effects on mental health. Victims may experience anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, and in severe cases, suicidal thoughts. It's essential to understand the emotional toll and work to prevent and stop cyberbullying.

How to Prevent Cyberbullying

  • Raise Awareness: Educate both children and adults on the harmful effects of cyberbullying.
  • Report Abuse: Make sure to report incidents of cyberbullying on social media platforms and websites.
  • Empower Victims: Encourage victims to seek help and provide them with resources to protect themselves.

Take Action

If you or someone you know is being cyberbullied, there are several steps you can take:

  • Report the bully to the platform or website where the bullying occurred.
  • Block or mute the person responsible for the harassment.
  • Seek professional help, such as speaking to a counselor or a trusted adult.
  • Document the bullying by taking screenshots and saving messages.
